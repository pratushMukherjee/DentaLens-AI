# Claims Process FAQ

## How do I file a dental claim?
- **In-network dentists** file claims electronically on your behalf. You typically don't need to do anything.
- **Out-of-network dentists:** Request an itemized statement from your dentist and submit a claim form to Delta Dental. Include the patient's name, member ID, dentist information, procedure codes (CDT codes), dates of service, and charges.

## How long does it take to process a claim?
Most claims are processed within 10-15 business days of receipt. Electronic claims submitted by in-network dentists are often processed faster, typically within 5-7 business days.

## What does each claim status mean?
- **Submitted:** Your claim has been received and is in the processing queue.
- **Pending:** Your claim is being reviewed. Additional information may be needed.
- **Approved:** Your claim has been approved and payment is being processed.
- **Denied:** Your claim was not approved. You will receive an Explanation of Benefits (EOB) explaining the reason.
- **Appealed:** You or your dentist has submitted an appeal for a denied claim.

## Why was my claim denied?
Common reasons for claim denials include:
- The procedure is not covered under your plan
- You have not met the waiting period for the service category
- You have reached your annual maximum
- The service exceeds frequency limitations (e.g., more than 2 cleanings per year)
- The procedure requires pre-authorization that was not obtained
- Missing or incorrect information on the claim

## How do I appeal a denied claim?
1. Review the Explanation of Benefits (EOB) to understand the denial reason
2. Gather supporting documentation (X-rays, clinical notes, treatment plans)
3. Submit a written appeal to Delta Dental within 180 days of the denial
4. Include your member ID, claim number, and a clear explanation of why the claim should be reconsidered
5. Your dentist can submit a peer-to-peer review request if the denial was based on clinical necessity

## What is an Explanation of Benefits (EOB)?
An EOB is a document sent to you after a claim is processed. It shows the services performed, the dentist's charges, the amount covered by your plan, and your share of the cost. It is not a bill — it's a summary of how your claim was processed.

## What is pre-authorization?
Pre-authorization (also called pre-determination) is a review of proposed dental treatment before it is performed. Your dentist submits a treatment plan, and Delta Dental confirms what will be covered and at what level. Pre-authorization is recommended for major services exceeding $300.

## Can I track my claims online?
Yes. Log in to your Delta Dental member portal to view claim status, EOBs, remaining benefits, and your deductible status in real time.
