# General Dental Insurance FAQ

## What is dental insurance?
Dental insurance is a type of health benefit that helps cover the cost of dental care. Plans typically cover preventive services at 100% and share costs for basic and major procedures through copays, coinsurance, or deductibles.

## What is the difference between PPO and HMO dental plans?
- **PPO (Preferred Provider Organization):** Offers a network of dentists at reduced rates. You can see any dentist, but pay less with in-network providers. Uses deductibles and coinsurance.
- **HMO (Health Maintenance Organization):** Requires you to choose a primary care dentist from the network. All care goes through that dentist. Uses fixed copays with no deductibles. Cannot see out-of-network dentists except for emergencies.

## What is an annual maximum?
The annual maximum is the most your dental plan will pay for covered services in a plan year. Once you reach the annual maximum, you are responsible for 100% of any remaining dental costs for that year. The maximum resets at the start of the next plan year.

## What is a deductible?
A deductible is the amount you pay out-of-pocket before your dental plan begins paying its share of covered services. Preventive services are typically exempt from the deductible.

## What is coinsurance?
Coinsurance is the percentage of costs you share with your dental plan after meeting your deductible. For example, if your plan covers basic services at 80%, you pay 20% coinsurance.

## How often can I get my teeth cleaned?
Most dental plans cover two prophylaxis (cleaning) appointments per calendar year. Some plans with periodontal benefits may cover additional periodontal maintenance visits.

## Are pre-existing conditions covered?
Dental plans generally do not exclude pre-existing conditions, but some services may have waiting periods. Check your specific plan for waiting period details.

## What should I do in a dental emergency?
Contact your dentist immediately. If after hours, most dental offices have an emergency line. If you have an HMO plan and cannot reach your assigned dentist, you may seek emergency care from any licensed dentist — emergency services are typically covered regardless of network status.
