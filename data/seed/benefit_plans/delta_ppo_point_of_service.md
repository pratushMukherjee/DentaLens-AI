# Delta Dental PPO Point-of-Service Plan

## Plan Overview
- **Plan Type:** Delta Dental PPO (Point-of-Service)
- **Networks:** Dual network — Delta Dental PPO network AND Delta Dental Premier network
- **Unique Advantage:** No other carrier offers this dual-network structure
- **Balance Billing:** Members are protected from balance billing with in-network providers
- **Flexibility:** Members can see any licensed dentist but save more with in-network providers

## How It Works
The Point-of-Service plan provides access to two networks in one program:
1. **Delta Dental PPO Network** — Highest savings, dentists accept reduced fees
2. **Delta Dental Premier Network** — Additional savings layer, broader network
3. **Out-of-Network** — Any licensed dentist, but member pays the difference between the dentist's charge and the allowed amount

## Cost Savings Example
- Dentist submits a $950 charge for a crown
- In-network PPO dentist accepts reduced fee of $744
- Immediate savings to member: $206
- Delta Dental then pays a percentage of the already reduced $744 fee

## Coverage Levels
- **Diagnostic & Preventive Services:** 100% coverage with PPO dentists, no deductible, no waiting period
- **Basic Services:** 80% after deductible
- **Major Services:** 50% after deductible

## Out-of-Pocket Maximums (Essential Health Benefits)
- Single coverage: $375 per benefit year
- Two or more covered persons: $750 per benefit year
- Counts toward OOP max: coinsurance, copayments, deductibles, and other out-of-pocket expenses for in-network EHB services
- Does NOT count toward OOP max: premiums, non-covered services, out-of-network dentist charges

## Key Benefits
- Two layers of cost savings and balance-billing protection in one program
- The nation's largest provider network of dentists across U.S. and Puerto Rico
- No waiting periods for diagnostic and preventive care
- Low deductible (applies only to basic and major services)
- 24/7 access to Member Portal at memberportal.com
- Mobile app for on-the-go benefits access

## Common Exclusions
- Correction of congenital or developmental malformations
- Cosmetic surgery (including facings posterior to second bicuspid)
- Treatment by anyone other than a licensed dentist or dental hygienist
- Veneers
- Prefabricated crowns as final restoration on permanent teeth
- Paste-type root canal fillings on permanent teeth
- Appliances or procedures for increasing vertical dimension
- Periodontal splinting
- Lost, missing, or stolen appliances
- Services not in the policy

## Coordination of Benefits
- Applies when a person is covered by more than one plan (e.g., both spouses have employer coverage)
- Goal: ensure combined payment does not exceed total claim amount
- When Delta Dental is primary: pays full benefit allowed by contract as if no other coverage exists
- When Delta Dental is secondary: pays up to but not exceeding what it would have paid as primary, or the total claim amount
