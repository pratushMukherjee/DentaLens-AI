# Coverage FAQ

## What dental services are typically covered?
Dental plans organize services into categories with different coverage levels:
- **Preventive & Diagnostic (100%):** Exams, cleanings, X-rays, fluoride treatments, sealants
- **Basic (70-80%):** Fillings, root canals, periodontal treatment, simple extractions
- **Major (40-50%):** Crowns, bridges, dentures, surgical extractions
- **Orthodontic (50%):** Braces and orthodontic treatment (not all plans include this)

## Are dental implants covered?
Standard Delta Dental plans do not cover dental implants. However, some plans offer a Major Services rider that includes implant coverage. Contact your benefits administrator to see if this option is available.

## Is teeth whitening covered?
No. Teeth whitening is considered a cosmetic procedure and is not covered under dental insurance plans. This includes both in-office bleaching and take-home whitening kits prescribed by a dentist.

## Are wisdom teeth extractions covered?
Yes. Wisdom tooth extractions are covered under Basic Services. Simple extractions (D7140) are covered when the tooth is erupted. Surgical extractions (D7210) are covered when the tooth is impacted or requires surgical removal.

## How does coverage work for children vs. adults?
- Children may receive additional preventive services like fluoride treatments and sealants
- Fluoride coverage typically has an age limit (under 14-16 depending on the plan)
- Sealants are typically covered only for permanent molars in children
- Orthodontic coverage, when available, covers both adolescents and adults

## What if I need a procedure that requires multiple visits?
Multi-visit procedures (like root canals or crown placement) are covered as a single procedure. The CDT code and coverage apply to the complete treatment, regardless of the number of visits required.

## Do I need a referral to see a specialist?
- **PPO plans:** No referral needed. You can see any specialist directly, though in-network specialists will cost less.
- **HMO plans:** Yes, your primary care dentist must refer you to an in-network specialist.

## What happens if I exceed my annual maximum?
Once you reach your annual maximum, you are responsible for 100% of any additional dental costs for the remainder of the plan year. The maximum resets on January 1st of the next plan year. Consider scheduling non-urgent major procedures in the next plan year if you're approaching your maximum.

## Are second opinions covered?
Yes. You can seek a second opinion from another dentist. The evaluation visit is covered as a periodic or comprehensive oral evaluation under your preventive benefits.

## What is the difference between "covered" and "allowed amount"?
- **Covered:** The procedure is eligible for benefits under your plan
- **Allowed amount:** The maximum fee Delta Dental will consider for a procedure. In-network dentists accept this as full payment. Out-of-network dentists may charge more, and you'd be responsible for the difference (balance billing).
